Kids Corner — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026. This policy covers the Kids Corner web app and the Kids Corner Guardian browser extension.

Kids Corner is a family tool a grown-up sets up for their own children. It is designed for privacy: a family's data lives in that family's own private space and is visible only to the grown-ups who set it up. We do not sell data, we do not show ads, and we do not use third-party advertising or analytics trackers.

Who this is for (children under 13 / COPPA)

Kids Corner is intended to be set up and managed by a parent or guardian for their own children. The grown-up provides any information about their children (a first name, an avatar, a login PIN) and controls it. Children do not create accounts and are not asked for personal information. A parent can review and delete their family's data at any time from the Grown-Ups dashboard.

What the Guardian extension collects

When a grown-up installs the Kids Corner Guardian on a child's device, the extension records — on that device — a small, aggregate summary of learning activity so the parent can see time on task:

  • Time on each allowed app (active minutes, idle time excluded).
  • Which allowed apps were opened and roughly when (time of day).
  • Blocked attempts — the domain of a site the child tried to reach that wasn't on the allow-list (e.g. youtube.com), so a parent can add it if it's needed.

The extension does not read page contents, keystrokes, passwords, form data, or full web addresses, and it collects nothing on sites that aren't part of a child's Kids Corner apps. It holds no login credentials of its own.

How the data is stored and who can see it

The activity summary is sent to the family's own private database (Google Firebase) through the signed-in Kids Corner page and is visible only to the grown-ups of that family, in their dashboard. It is never shared with other families, sold, or used for advertising.

Permissions the extension uses, and why

  • Read and change data on websites — to redirect the browser away from sites a grown-up hasn't allowed, and to see which allowed app is in the foreground for time-on-task.
  • Storage / alarms / idle / tabs — to keep the allow-list, measure active (not idle) time, and know the current tab.

Your choices

A grown-up can uninstall the extension at any time, and can delete their family's activity data from the dashboard. To request deletion or ask a question, contact the family administrator who set up your Kids Corner, or email chris.aro@nucleos.com.
